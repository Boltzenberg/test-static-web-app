ARCHIVAL DISCOVERIES

Lost Diary of Snow White Found in Cottage Attic,
Confirms She Was "Basically Running Everything"

By Rumpelstiltskin R. Flintwick, Staff Correspondent  |  Enchanted Forest Bureau

A personal diary believed to belong to Snow White — the celebrated princess, amateur naturalist, and full-time cottage manager — has been recovered from a cedar chest in the attic of a seven-bedroom dwelling in the Enchanted Forest, conservators at the Enchanted Forest Archives confirmed this week.

The diary, which spans several weeks of what archivists believe was a particularly eventful November and December, was discovered during a routine assessment of the property following the recent relocation of its occupants to a larger residence. It was found wrapped in a piece of linen, tucked beneath a broken accordion and what one archivist described as "an alarming number of empty tonic bottles."

"We knew immediately what we had," said Dr. Fauna Bramblewood, Senior Curator of Enchanted Ephemera at the Archives. "The handwriting is consistent with known samples. The chamomile stains on folios seven through eleven are, frankly, a provenance dream."

"The chamomile stains on folios seven through eleven are, frankly, a provenance dream."
— Dr. Fauna Bramblewood, Senior Curator

The diary offers an intimate window into daily life in the cottage during a period that scholars have long described as "the quiet years" — the stretch between Snow White's famous awakening and her eventual departure for the palace. What emerges is a portrait of a young woman navigating, with considerable patience and the occasional cutting aside, a household in a state of sustained interpersonal chaos.

🍎 📖 ✨
The diary, still faintly smelling of chamomile and what curators believe may be venison stew, is now on display in the Enchanted Forest Archives' Reading Room. Visitors are reminded not to touch the pages. (Enchanted Forest Archives / Staff)

The entries document, among other things, a series of feuds among the cottage's seven dwarf residents — described by Snow White with the weary affection of someone who has read the same argument many times. There is the noise complaint. There is the awards ceremony. There is an incident involving a medicinal tonic, a filing cabinet, and a sneeze of apparently historic proportions.

"She clearly adored them," said Dr. Bramblewood. "But she was also, very obviously, the only competent adult in that building."

Perhaps most remarkably, the later pages of the diary reveal Snow White turning her attention to an unexpected subject — one that curators say they did not anticipate finding in a personal journal of this period. Details, however, will have to wait.

"We're letting the diary speak for itself," Dr. Bramblewood said. "Start at the beginning. It earns its ending."

the whole gang

It is November, and the leaves are down, and the cottage smells of wood smoke and whatever Dopey knocked over this morning. (Soup, I think. The ceiling is involved.) I want to record who lives here, for my own peace of mind, because I find it helpful to remember that I love them.

Seven dwarfs. I love every single one of them. They go to the mine each morning, they come home, they eat my cooking, and — mostly — they get along. It is a good life. A quiet life.

Nothing could possibly go wrong.

2
November 10th — After Dinner 😤
the whistling incident
😤 vs 😊   The Whistling Incident

Something has happened. Grumpy has officially declared war on Happy, and I am caught in the middle holding a dish towel.

It started, as most things do, in the mine. Happy was whistling — which, yes, he does constantly, but he has always done this, and it has always been fine, and the birds like it. But today Grumpy was trying to concentrate on a particularly stubborn vein of sapphires, and Happy was right beside him whistling "Hi-Ho" in a cheerful loop, and something in Grumpy just… snapped.

He filed a formal noise complaint. In writing. With a wax seal. He slid it under my bedroom door at six in the morning. It was three pages long and referenced "psychological damages" and "loss of sapphire productivity estimated at four carats." I could not figure out where he got the wax.

Happy, when I told him, laughed so hard he cried. Which I had to admit was also a bit funny. But I did not say that.

I told Grumpy I would "look into it." I have no intention of looking into it. (This is called diplomacy.)

Official Tally:
Noise complaints filed: 7
Happy's reaction each time: 😂
Grumpy's reaction to that: 😤😤

I worry about this. Grumpy has always been grumpy — it is in the name, it is practically a profession — but there is a new edge to it lately. Like he is one whistled chorus away from something irreversible. I hope it passes.

3
November 17th — Evening 🕯️
three years of grudge
😴 vs 😬   The Awards Ceremony Catastrophe

Bashful is not speaking to Sleepy again. Or rather, Bashful is not speaking to Sleepy still, because this is not a new feud, this is a three-year-old feud that has never been resolved and probably never will be.

What happened was: the Enchanted Forest Gem-of-the-Year Awards, three years ago November. Bashful had been nominated in the category of "Outstanding Discovery: Moonstone." He had prepared a speech. He had practiced the speech. He told me about it in advance, twice, and I could tell he was nervous and proud in equal measure.

He won. He went up to the podium. He began his speech — and there, in the front row, Sleepy fell completely asleep. Audibly. The snoring was described by multiple attendees as "resonant." The award presenter paused mid-sentence. A bird landed on Sleepy's hat and stayed there for the entire acceptance speech.

Bashful blushed so hard his hat slid down over his eyes. He has not forgiven Sleepy. Sleepy, for his part, says he "remembers it fondly" because he had a very nice dream during Bashful's speech, which is genuinely the worst thing he could have said.

"It was a lovely dream." — Sleepy
"I will never forgive him." — Bashful

I tried to raise it with Sleepy at breakfast. He blinked at me slowly and said, "Has it really been three years?" and then fell asleep in his porridge. I am choosing to interpret this as remorse.

The cottage feels tenser than usual. Grumpy is still filing complaints. Now Bashful won't sit in the same room as Sleepy. Meals are becoming a logistical puzzle. I am starting to think someone needs to do something before this all calcifies permanently.

4
November 24th — I Can't Even 🫖
the big one
🤧 vs 🤓 vs 💤   The Tonic Catastrophe

I don't even know where to begin. I'm going to need more tea. (I'm already on my third cup.)

Doc had been working for weeks on what he called a "proprietary sneeze-suppression tonic" for Sneezy, who, bless him, had recently sneezed during a very sensitive gemstone appraisal and sent six emeralds down the back of the fireplace. The tonic was apparently very delicate — Doc had it in a small brown bottle on the kitchen shelf, next to the cooking sherry, labeled "FOR SNEEZY — DO NOT DRINK."

Dopey drank it. He thought it was the cooking sherry. Nobody can explain this because the label was very clear. Dopey says he cannot read cursive. This is possibly true.

What followed was the single most catastrophic sneeze in recorded cottage history. Dopey, now in possession of months of Sneezy's suppressed sneezing in one concentrated medicinal dose, sneezed so hard and for so long that he destroyed Doc's entire filing cabinet. Seven years of medical notes. Gone. Scattered across the kitchen like the world's most depressing confetti.

Doc is furious at Dopey for drinking it. Sneezy is furious at Doc for creating a tonic that, as Sneezy put it, "clearly does not work and actually makes everything worse." Dopey is furious at no one because Dopey lacks the emotional architecture for sustained fury, but he did look a bit sheepish for almost twenty minutes.

None of the three are speaking. The infirmary smells of elderflower, regret, and what I believe are the remnants of Doc's notes on seasonal rhinitis.

I have now attempted to mediate three separate feuds in three weeks. I am one person. I made venison stew tonight and no one mentioned it, because Grumpy was glaring at the wall, Bashful was visibly trembling with suppressed feelings, and Doc, Sneezy, and Dopey were arranged at maximum distance from each other like the points of a very unhappy triangle.

The cottage that was once warm and full of whistling and small disasters is now just full of small disasters. I need to do something. I just don't know what yet.

5
December 1st — After Much Reflection 🎄
the idea!!

I have been thinking about this for a week. What do you do when seven people you love have quietly stopped being kind to each other? You cannot force it. You cannot lecture it. Grumpy once responded to a lecture of mine by submitting a counter-complaint about the lecture.

But here is what I know: people are at their most generous in December. Something about the cold and the candles and the smell of pine makes even the most entrenched grudge feel a little less worth maintaining. It is not permanent, but it is real.

So. I am going to host Secret Santa.

The idea is this: everyone draws a name, everyone buys one gift, everyone sits together by the fire on Christmas Eve and — for one evening at least — has to be gracious about it. It forces proximity without requiring apology. It creates a shared project. It is gentle, it is festive, and most importantly, it is structured enough that Grumpy cannot file a complaint against it.

The only complication is the feuds. I cannot have Grumpy draw Happy — he would buy him something passive-aggressive and everyone would know it. Same for Sleepy and Bashful. And putting any two of the tonic trio together would be a disaster.

So I will simply build that into the rules. Keep the feuding pairs away from each other. The hat can handle the rest.

I am going to propose it at dinner tomorrow. I am cautiously optimistic. (I am terrified. But cautiously.)

6
December 2nd — Evening 🥣
the hat!!

It worked. I proposed Secret Santa at dinner and nobody walked out. Grumpy said it was "a waste of perfectly good paper" but he took a slip, so. I am counting that as enthusiasm.

I wrote all seven names on slips — just the dwarfs, I will coordinate from outside the hat this year — and we passed it around after soup. The rules, as I announced them:

① No one draws themselves. That is not a gift, that is just shopping.
② No feuding pairs. To keep the peace and protect the gifts.
— Grumpy ↔ Happy: forbidden
— Sleepy ↔ Bashful: forbidden
— Sneezy, Doc & Dopey: none of the three may draw either of the others

Everyone drew a name. We had apple cider. Happy whistled one bar of something and stopped himself. Growth. Bashful smiled at least twice. Even Doc seemed, if not warm, then at least room temperature.

I went to bed feeling genuinely pleased. And then I couldn't sleep because something felt off. Something mathematical.

Wait. The constraint groups are asymmetric — two separate pairs of two, plus one group of three. Does each dwarf actually have an equal chance of drawing any of their eligible recipients? Or does the structure of the rules quietly tilt the odds?

I got out of bed and started counting.

7
December 2nd — 2:00 in the morning 🕑
couldn't sleep

Made chamomile. First cup. Started listing every possible way the names could have been drawn. Just the seven dwarfs for now, since I'm coordinating from outside the hat this year.

①Seven people, all drawing different names. Total possible arrangements: 7! = 5,040.
②Now cross out any arrangement where someone draws themselves, or draws a feud-partner. Each forbidden pair eliminates a whole swathe of arrangements.
③After all the crossing out…
288
legal arrangements survive. Out of 5,040.

Now the question keeping me up: within those 288, does each dwarf have an equal shot at drawing any person they're eligible for?

If fair: each eligible name should appear equally often across all 288. But the constraint groups aren't the same size. This seems suspicious. 🍵

December 2nd — 4:00 in the morning 🕓
!!!

Third cup. Here is the part that kept me staring at the ceiling: it is not that everyone has unequal odds. The unfairness is specific.

😤😊 Grumpy & Happy · 😴😬 Sleepy & Bashful
— UNFAIR

Each has 5 eligible people. But the odds are not 1-in-5 across the board:

other pair member: 12.5% trio member: 25%

The feuding trio is twice as likely to end up in your draw as the other pair. Structurally biased, invisibly, from a hat that looked perfectly innocent.

🤧🤓💤 Sneezy, Doc & Dopey
— accidentally fair??

Each has 4 eligible people — and draws all four with exactly equal odds:

each pair-member: 25%

Their eligible pool is symmetrically constrained with respect to each other, so the bias cancels. They got fairness for free. Without deserving it, I might add.

I cannot believe Sneezy, Doc, and Dopey — the men responsible for the Great Tonic Catastrophe — have the most statistically equitable outcomes in this cottage.

10
December 2nd — 5:00 in the morning 🕔
the maths of it

Fourth cup. Trying to put into words why this happens, in case I ever need to explain it to someone. (I won't. But just in case.)

🔗

Forbidden draws make everything quietly correlated

When you rule out certain pairings, you don't just remove those outcomes — you shift the weight of all the remaining 288. Every dwarf's draw is invisibly tied to everyone else's, like a very tense game of musical chairs.

⚖️

The trio can only be drawn by the four pair-members — and that's the whole problem.

Sneezy, Doc, and Dopey are blocked from drawing each other, so their names must be drawn by someone from the two pairs — Grumpy, Happy, Sleepy, or Bashful. All three trio slots flow exclusively to those four people. Meanwhile, Sleepy and Bashful are also blocked from each other, so those two names compete for fewer valid drawers as well. The result: when you're one of the four pair-members, you're far more likely to end up drawing a trio member — who has nowhere else to go — than the other pair, whose slots are more constrained. Exactly two-to-one more likely, as it turns out.

🎲

A fair hat draw ≠ a fair outcome

Our hat picked uniformly from the 288 valid arrangements — all equally likely. But "each arrangement is equally likely" is not the same as "each person has equal odds of drawing each eligible recipient." True fairness would need deliberately balanced cycles, not rejection sampling. Which is a lot to ask on a Tuesday in December.

11
December 3rd — After Breakfast ☕
the conversation

Doc cornered me in the kitchen this morning. He had a look on his face that I've come to recognize — the one that means "I have been thinking about something and I am about to be both right and annoying about it." He also had the look of someone who had not slept. At all. Honestly, without the glasses, he was giving quite a strong Dopey impression. I did not say this.

DOC 🤓
Snow White, I've been thinking about the Secret Santa draw. Something feels… off. I can't quite put my finger on it, but intuitively, with our particular set of restrictions, I don't think the probability distribution is uniform across eligible recipients. The asymmetry of the constraint groups seems like it would…
SNOW WHITE 👸
Yes, Doc. I know.
DOC 🤓
You — you know? I was up all night with this. Every hour. I have pages of notes. Have you already worked it out?
SNOW WHITE 👸
I worked it out by 5am. Sit down. So: there are 288 valid arrangements. The hat draws uniformly from those 288. But "uniform over arrangements" is not the same as "uniform over eligible recipients." Because Grumpy — for example — can draw five people, but the arrangements where he draws Sleepy or Bashful are rarer than those where he draws you, Sneezy, or Dopey. The reason is that Sleepy and Bashful are also blocked from each other, which makes those arrangements harder to complete. Your group — you three — are only blocked from each other, so your "slots" are easier to fill, making those arrangements more common. So Grumpy's draw tilts toward your group at 25% each, while Sleepy and Bashful only come up at 12.5% each. Exactly half as often.
DOC 🤓
Right. Yes. That's… that's exactly what I was going to say. And ironically, our group — my group — we actually draw the four pair-members at perfectly equal odds?
SNOW WHITE 👸
Perfectly equal. 25% each. You're the only fair ones in the whole draw. By accident. Because your eligible pool happens to be symmetric. Unlike everyone else's.
DOC 🤓
…I have to say, 5am is quite impressive. That's… yes. Well done.
12
December 3rd — Evening 🎄
the moral

I told no one else. The gifts were exchanged. The cider was consumed. Grumpy said his gift was "acceptable," which from him is the equivalent of a standing ovation. Bashful blushed when he opened his — pleasantly, for once. Even Sleepy managed to stay awake for the unwrapping, which is more than he did for the awards ceremony.

But I keep thinking about what the numbers revealed. A draw that feels random — everyone pulling a folded slip from the same hat — can still be quietly, structurally biased. Not because anyone cheated. Not because the hat was rigged. But because the shape of the constraints creates an uneven landscape of valid outcomes, and some names just appear more often in that landscape than others.

288

valid draws out of 5,040 possible arrangements

2×

more likely to draw a trio member than the other pair

4 of 7

dwarfs had quietly unfair odds from a "random" hat

Random ≠ Fair. The structure of your rules shapes the space of your outcomes. And that space is rarely as equal as it looks from the outside.

Next year I am constructing a deliberately balanced assignment cycle. Or just buying everyone the same candle. (Beeswax. They'd like it.)

— Snow White xo
